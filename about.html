This past summer, Lizzie, a CS student at Bryn Mawr College, was an 8th grade Algebra Teaching Assistant through Silicon Valley Education Foundation, teaching rising East Palo Alto eighth graders Algebra. Though difficult, it was rewarding because of a few students who truly cared about learning. Here, Lizzie got the idea of a remote, simple "mentorship" program and online database of mentors to guide a few eighth graders from the program through high school, and then through college. These students had the drive to focus, study, and learn, while some of their peers did not. Despite peer pressure and many distractions, these students made going to work enjoyable, and their dedication to their education will get them far. Edify101is here to help them go far--they have the mindset, but to answer any questions about college or high school, here we are.

This New York Times  article explains how a simple, weekly text to a student can make them 70% more likely to go to college. Edify101 aims to provide mentors to listen, advise, and provide support, whether it be email or phone call, to both the student and the family. A little bit goes a long way, and in order to thrive in school, it takes an army.

Edify101 provides an online database of mentors (high school, college, or career professional volunteers) to suggest courses, extracurriculars, provide support, and more. If a student has a question, or needs someone to talk to, this program will give them that option, that chance, that opportunity. Maybe they have no one to talk to about college, about academics, no one to answer their many questions. We hope to also provide SAT and ACT tutoring, as well as college advising.
